INFO!

 

Jasmine Roberts is an Engineer, Designer and Researcher working with new technologies and exploring possible futures. Her research focuses on synthetic realities, virtual humans, living machines, and perceptual and cognitive systems. Her work is comprised of computer vision and graphics, machine learning, mathematical modeling of physics and spatial design.
Most recently, Jasmine finished graduate studies at the MIT Media Lab and the Harvard Graduate School of Design and has contributed to interaction methods for PlayStation VR and Intel's Real Sense Depth and Tracking Cameras, and Unity Labs' MARS Project. Jasmine has also worked with NASA, Google, Mozilla, Microsoft and Oculus. Jasmine completed her undergraduate studies in applied physics (materials science) and electrical engineering at Columbia University in 2014.
She has won numerous academic research awards, speaks publicly at AR/VR/MR conferences, the United Nations. In addition to lecturing at her alma maters, Jasmine is guest lecturer at the Strelka Institute, UC Berkeley and Stanford University.
Jasmine is currently working with various organizations as a facilitator and organizer, bringing her knoweldge in Spatial Computing, Non-Visual Interfaces, Perception, Sensing and emerging areas of design interactions.
